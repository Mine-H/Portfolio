From the author

Hi! I am Henrique, a graduate at the University of Staffordshire with a passion for programming, ears tuned to game audio, and a heart for games designing.

I've programmed as a hobby for a while now, which over the last half-decade has led me to learning languages from JavaScript to Python just for the fun of it. I've also played games since I was very young, however, my first encounter with the concept of making a game was with a small showcase a town over from where I lived at the time. Their team was presenting Construct 2 as an introduction to game-making, and from there I moved to coding Discord bots in Python and static HTML webpages, then learnt Unity in parallel with my A-levels before learning Unreal in my first year of university.

I owe a lot of my success with programming to my A-level Computer Science teacher, who gave a lot of support to my interest in the subject and showed me examples of previous years' end-of-year videogame projects as inspiration and guidance. This interest in creating videogames brought me to make my own as my Computer Science project using Unity. You might've seen a screenshot of it already, as I used that game as the background for the homepage's title screen.

In terms of music and audio: I've sung and played the piano for over a decade, practised the accordion since May '25, and gave a shot at both brass, strings (strummed and bowed), wood, and percussion instruments. My current tool of music composition is a mixture of piano/humming "drafts"/"sketching" followed by a MIDI arrangement fit to the game's theme.

Outside of Work

I have a sweetspot for Tabletop RPGs. Some friends and I meet online every month to play a Pathfinder 2e one-shot session; and I was an active member in the weekly DnD one-shots held by one of the university's societies.

Every year, I challenge myself to make a prop weapon or armour out of cardboard and glue to showcase on Halloween. Last year, I managed to outline a bike helmet's edges onto the cardboard, adjust a prototype to fit my head, cut it out and glue it all together. One of my favourite crafts from this has been a large cardboard battleaxe, with custom detailing on the axe-head and somewhat realistic weight-balancing. As of writing (June '26), the cardboard helmet is still in shape and has only required re-gluing three times.

My attempts at improving my art skills have slowly gotten better. It isn't often, but sometimes when I need to pass the time I'll take my pencil and book out and sketch. My art style appears to be more suited for inanimate objects or fantasy creatures, as I am yet to have a good grasp of anatomy, and I often lean into realism (especially for drawing trees and shadows). I do find it difficult to colour my sketches in, as my imagination is often better than my skills with a paintbrush, so I lean into the monochrome style and do my best with rough outlines and grey shadows.

I often spend my free time either playing videogames or watching videos from science communicators. My focus recently has been in Satisfactory and astronomy respectively.
